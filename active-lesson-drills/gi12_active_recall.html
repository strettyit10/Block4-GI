Practice MCQs — GI12 Embryology
Q1. A surgeon identifies the hepatoduodenal ligament during laparoscopic cholecystectomy. Which embryological structure gives rise to this ligament?



B — Ventral mesentery. The hepatoduodenal ligament = free edge of the lesser omentum (ventral mesentery derivative). It contains the portal triad: portal vein (posterior), hepatic artery proper (left), CBD (right). Pringle maneuver = compress this ligament to control hemorrhage. Foramen of Winslow lies posterior to it.
Q2. CT scan shows a retroperitoneal hematoma surrounding the duodenum after blunt trauma. Which BEST explains why the duodenum is in a retroperitoneal position?



C — Secondarily retroperitoneal. Duodenum starts intraperitoneal with mesentery. During stomach rotation and C-loop formation, mesentery fuses with posterior peritoneum → 2nd, 3rd, 4th parts = secondarily retroperitoneal. The 1st part (duodenal bulb/cap) remains intraperitoneal — most common site for duodenal ulcers. Retroperitoneal perforation → retroperitoneal air, not immediate peritonitis.
Q3. During lateral folding, the lateral plate mesoderm splits. The SPACE between the two resulting layers becomes which structure?



C — Intraembryonic coelom. Lateral plate mesoderm splits into somatic (parietal) layer = lines body wall, and splanchnic (visceral) layer = surrounds gut. The space = intraembryonic coelom, which subdivides into pericardial, pleural, and peritoneal cavities.
Q4. A surgical resident is about to perform a truncal vagotomy. The attending says: "Use anterior and posterior, not left and right." Which embryological event explains this instruction?



B — Stomach 90° clockwise → left vagus becomes anterior vagal trunk. Original left surface → anterior; right surface → posterior. At the GEJ, "left" and "right" no longer apply — always say anterior/posterior vagal trunk. Cutting only one trunk = incomplete vagotomy → recurrent ulcers.
Q5. Newborn A: bowel at umbilicus covered by a translucent sac; trisomy 18. Newborn B: bowel right of umbilicus, no sac, normal chromosomes. Which correctly distinguishes them?



B — Omphalocele (A) vs. Gastroschisis (B). Omphalocele = failure of intestinal retraction → sac present (peritoneum + amnion) → at umbilicus → Trisomies 13/18, Beckwith-Wiedemann. Gastroschisis = lateral wall closure defect → no sac → right of umbilicus → isolated, no chromosomal link → good prognosis. The sac in omphalocele is PROTECTIVE (not a source of infection).
Q6. A 4-year-old boy has painless bright red rectal bleeding. Tc-99m pertechnetate scan shows uptake 2 feet from the ileocecal valve. Which tissue is responsible for the bleeding?



C — Ectopic gastric mucosa. Meckel diverticulum (vitelline duct remnant). Rule of 2s: 2% of population, ~2 inches long, ~2 feet from ICV, presents within first 2 years. Ectopic gastric mucosa secretes HCl → ulcerates adjacent ileum → painless rectal bleeding. Tc-99m pertechnetate taken up by gastric mucosa (normal + ectopic) = "Meckel scan."
Q7. A 3-day-old male has not passed meconium; abdominal distension; digital rectal exam causes explosive stool release. Barium enema: narrow rectosigmoid with dilated proximal colon. Which histological finding is diagnostic?



C — Absent ganglion cells + hypertrophied nerve trunks. Gold standard: suction rectal biopsy of the NARROW DISTAL (aganglionic) segment showing (1) absence of ganglion cells in both Meissner and Auerbach plexuses, (2) hypertrophied acetylcholinesterase-positive nerve trunks. Calretinin staining highlights ganglion cells — absent in Hirschsprung. Biopsying the dilated proximal bowel gives false negative (it has normal ganglia).
Q8. A 6-week-old male has non-bilious projectile vomiting since age 3 weeks. Hungry immediately after vomiting. ABG: pH 7.51, HCO₃⁻ 37, K⁺ 2.9, Cl⁻ 88. Most appropriate NEXT STEP after confirming the diagnosis?



B — Correct electrolytes FIRST, then surgery. Hypertrophic pyloric stenosis → hypokalemic hypochloremic metabolic alkalosis (HCl loss in vomitus + secondary renal K⁺ loss). Surgery is NOT emergent. Alkalosis must be corrected (IV NS + KCl) before anesthesia — alkalosis impairs anesthetic metabolism and shifts oxyhemoglobin curve. Then: Ramstedt pyloromyotomy. Confirm with US: muscle thickness >4 mm, channel >14 mm.
