Practice Questions
Q1: Acid suppression mechanism
A 38-year-old man with erosive GERD is started on omeprazole. Which of the following best describes the mechanism of this drug?

A) Reversible blockade of H₂ histamine receptors on parietal cells
B) Irreversible blockade of the H⁺/K⁺-ATPase (proton pump) in parietal cell canaliculus
C) Neutralization of existing gastric acid via alkaline ions
D) Inhibition of gastrin release from G cells
E) Competitive blockade of H⁺/K⁺-ATPase (reversible)
Correct Answer: B

Omeprazole is a proton pump inhibitor (PPI) that irreversibly blockades the H⁺/K⁺-ATPase. This is the final common pathway for acid secretion, making PPIs the most potent acid suppressors. They are activated in the acidic canalicular space and bind irreversibly to the pump. A is H₂ blockers (reversible). C is antacids. D is not a mechanism of any major acid-suppressive drug. E describes P-CABs, which are newer.
Q2: H. pylori eradication regimen
A 45-year-old woman is diagnosed with H. pylori by urea breath test and presents with a peptic ulcer. She is prescribed quadruple therapy. Which of the following is the correct combination?

A) Amoxicillin + Clarithromycin + PPI
B) Metronidazole + Tetracycline + PPI + Bismuth subsalicylate
C) Levofloxacin + Clarithromycin + H₂ blocker
D) Amoxicillin + Doxycycline + PPI + Antacid
E) Triple therapy is sufficient; quadruple is not standard
Correct Answer: B

Quadruple therapy (the current standard per Medical Letter) consists of: Metronidazole + Tetracycline + PPI + Bismuth subsalicylate for 7–14 days. The PPI reduces acid (heals ulcer) AND improves antibiotic efficacy. Bismuth has intrinsic antimicrobial activity. A is older triple therapy (falling out of favor). C uses wrong drugs. D mixes different agents. E is incorrect; quadruple is now preferred due to clarithromycin resistance.
Q3: Antacid side effects
A patient is given aluminum hydroxide for 2 weeks to treat GERD symptoms. Which side effect would you expect?

A) Diarrhea and hypermagnesemia
B) Constipation and phosphate depletion
C) Hypercalcemia and bloating
D) Hypomagnesemia and loose stools
E) Metabolic alkalosis and muscle weakness
Correct Answer: B

Aluminum hydroxide causes CONSTIPATION (not diarrhea) and binds phosphate in the gut, leading to phosphate depletion. Magnesium hydroxide does the opposite (diarrhea). Remember: "Mg = Must Go" (diarrhea), "Al = All Locked Up" (constipation).
Q4: 5-HT3 antagonists and chemotherapy nausea
A 52-year-old woman receiving chemotherapy for breast cancer develops severe nausea. She is prescribed ondansetron IV before her next infusion. What is the mechanism of this drug?

A) Dopamine antagonist at the CTZ
B) Blocks 5-HT3 receptors, preventing vagal afferent signaling from GI to vomiting center
C) M1 muscarinic receptor antagonist (motion sickness pathway)
D) Increases gastric motility via 5-HT4 agonism
E) Reduces intracranial pressure in the CTZ
Correct Answer: B

Ondansetron (and other setrons like granisetron) are selective 5-HT3 receptor antagonists. They block 5-HT3 receptors on vagal afferent neurons, preventing the transmission of chemotherapy-induced nausea signals to the vomiting center. A is metoclopramide. C is scopolamine/antihistamines. D is cisapride/prucalopride (prokinetics). E is not a mechanism of any antiemetic.
Q5: Metoclopramide safety concern
A 40-year-old man with gastroparesis is treated with metoclopramide for 18 months at standard doses. He begins to develop involuntary orofacial movements. Which adverse effect is this consistent with?

A) Extrapyramidal side effects (acute dystonia)
B) Neuroleptic malignant syndrome
C) Tardive dyskinesia
D) Parkinsonism (tremor)
E) Akathisia
Correct Answer: C

Tardive dyskinesia (involuntary dyskinetic movements, especially orofacial) is a serious long-term risk of metoclopramide (a D2 blocker), especially with prolonged use (>3 months) at higher doses. This is an FDA black box warning. A/B/D/E are other dopamine antagonist side effects but occur acutely. The 18-month duration and specific presentation point to tardive dyskinesia.
Q6: Laxative onset classification
An elderly patient with constipation needs immediate bowel evacuation for a colonoscopy tomorrow morning. Which laxative has the fastest onset (hours)?

A) Psyllium husk (Metamucil)
B) Magnesium citrate (osmotic)
C) Docusate (stool softener)
D) Bisacodyl (stimulant)
E) Mineral oil
Correct Answer: B

Osmotic laxatives like magnesium citrate work within 2–3 hours by drawing water into the intestinal lumen. This makes them ideal for rapid bowel evacuation (prep). A (bulk) takes 12–24 hours. C (softener) is very slow. D (stimulant) takes 6–12 hours. E (mineral oil) takes 6–8 hours.
Q7: Antidiarrheal contraindication
A 35-year-old man presents with acute bloody diarrhea, fever, and positive stool culture for Shigella. He requests medication to stop the diarrhea. What is the best recommendation?

A) Prescribe loperamide (Imodium) to reduce symptoms
B) Prescribe diphenoxylate with atropine (Lomotil)
C) Avoid antidiarrheals; they worsen invasive bacterial infections by trapping organisms and toxins
D) Give bismuth subsalicylate for symptom relief
E) Both A and B are acceptable first-line treatments
Correct Answer: C

Antidiarrheals (loperamide, diphenoxylate) are CONTRAINDICATED in invasive bacterial diarrhea (Shigella, EAEC, Salmonella, Campylobacter) and toxin-mediated diarrhea (C. difficile, Enterotoxigenic E. coli) because they trap organisms/toxins in the gut, prolonging infection and worsening complications. Supportive care with ORS is preferred. D (bismuth) is less problematic but still used cautiously.
Q8: IBD drug choice — anti-TNF vs anti-IL-23
A 28-year-old woman is diagnosed with moderate Crohn's disease and is started on anti-TNF biologic therapy with infliximab. Which of the following best describes infliximab's role in IBD?

A) It blocks IL-23 signaling, preventing Th17 differentiation
B) It is a monoclonal antibody that binds TNF-α, preventing its interaction with TNFR1 and TNFR2, reducing pro-inflammatory cascade
C) It inhibits 5-HT4 receptors to reduce intestinal motility
D) It replaces missing 5-ASA at mucosal sites
E) It repairs the intestinal barrier by blocking zonula occludens proteins
Correct Answer: B

Infliximab is a chimeric monoclonal antibody against TNF-α. It binds TNF and prevents its interaction with tumor necrosis factor receptors, reducing the pro-inflammatory Th1/Th17 response in both GI and systemic sites. A describes anti-IL-23 agents like risankizumab (newer, also effective). C is antiemetic/prokinetic. D and E are not mechanisms of biologics.
