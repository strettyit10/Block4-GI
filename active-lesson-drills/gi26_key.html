LOs 1–4
Q1: Hikers and foul fatty diarrhea
32 F returns from camping where she drank untreated stream water. 3 weeks of foul-smelling greasy floating stools, bloating, weight loss.

A) Cryptosporidium; nitazoxanide
B) Giardia lamblia; metronidazole (or tinidazole)
C) Entamoeba histolytica; metronidazole + paromomycin
D) Cyclospora; TMP-SMX
E) Rotavirus; supportive
Correct Answer: B

Giardia — foul fatty diarrhea, hikers/daycare, untreated water. Treat with metronidazole or tinidazole.
Q2: HIV + watery diarrhea
38 M with AIDS (CD4 = 42) has 3 weeks of profuse watery diarrhea. Modified acid-fast stain shows 4–6 μm oocysts.

A) Giardia
B) Entamoeba
C) Cryptosporidium parvum
D) Cyclospora
E) Cystoisospora
Correct Answer: C

Cryptosporidium — small acid-fast oocysts; severe chronic in AIDS. Nitazoxanide limited; ART to restore CD4.
Q3: Chagas cardiomyopathy
52 M Bolivian with CHF and dysphagia. ECG: RBBB + LAFB. Echo: apical aneurysm. Megaesophagus.

A) T. brucei; tsetse
B) T. cruzi; reduviid bug
C) L. donovani; sandfly
D) Babesia; Ixodes
E) P. falciparum; Anopheles
Correct Answer: B

Chagas disease (T. cruzi), reduviid ("kissing") bug. Chronic cardiomyopathy with apical aneurysm + RBBB/LAFB + megaesophagus/colon. Benznidazole or nifurtimox.
Q4: Malaria relapse
Treated 3 months ago for P. vivax with chloroquine; returns with recurrent fever + parasitemia. Missed drug?

A) Atovaquone-proguanil
B) Mefloquine
C) Primaquine (after G6PD testing)
D) Doxycycline
E) Artesunate
Correct Answer: C

Vivax/ovale hypnozoites → relapse. Primaquine (or tafenoquine) kills hypnozoites — check G6PD first.
Q5: Strongyloides hyperinfection
60 M Vietnamese starts high-dose prednisone → bloody sputum, abdominal pain, E. coli bacteremia, filariform larvae in sputum.

A) Stool O&P and empiric ivermectin prior to steroids
B) Nothing
C) Blood smear for malaria
D) Colonoscopy
E) CXR only
Correct Answer: A

Strongyloides persists decades via autoinfection; steroids → hyperinfection with GNR sepsis and ARDS. Screen and treat with ivermectin before immunosuppression.
Q6: Neurocysticercosis
28 M from rural Mexico with new seizures. MRI: multiple cystic CNS lesions with scolex.

A) T. saginata; undercooked beef
B) T. solium; ingestion of eggs (fecal-oral)
C) Echinococcus
D) Toxocara
E) Toxoplasma
Correct Answer: B

Neurocysticercosis = ingestion of T. solium EGGS (not larvae). Albendazole + steroids + anticonvulsants.
Q7: B12 deficiency from fish
40 M sushi enthusiast with macrocytic anemia, ↑MMA, hypersegmented neutrophils. Stool O&P: operculated eggs.

A) Diphyllobothrium latum; competes for B12
B) Ascaris
C) Giardia
D) Whipple
E) Schistosoma
Correct Answer: A

Diphyllobothrium latum competes for B12 → megaloblastic anemia. Raw fish. Praziquantel.
Q8: Hydatid cyst
45 M sheep rancher with large cystic liver lesion, eggshell calcification, daughter cysts. Echinococcus serology +.

A) Percutaneous aspiration alone
B) Albendazole + PAIR or surgical resection (avoid spillage)
C) Metronidazole
D) Observation
E) Ivermectin
Correct Answer: B

Echinococcus — albendazole + PAIR or surgery. Spillage risks anaphylaxis and seeding.
Q9: Pinworm
6 y/o with nocturnal perianal itching; tape test shows oval eggs flattened on one side.

A) Topical steroids
B) Metronidazole
C) Albendazole or mebendazole for patient AND contacts; repeat in 2 wk
D) Ivermectin alone
E) Praziquantel
Correct Answer: C

Enterobius: albendazole or pyrantel; treat household; repeat in 2 weeks.
Q10: Schistosoma + bladder cancer
35 M Egyptian with painless hematuria; UA shows terminal-spine eggs; cystoscopy: SCC of bladder.

A) S. mansoni
B) S. japonicum
C) S. haematobium
D) S. mekongi
E) S. intercalatum
Correct Answer: C

S. haematobium → bladder → SCC (terminal-spine eggs). All treated with praziquantel.
Q11: Toxoplasma in AIDS
45 y/o with AIDS (CD4 = 50), headache, hemiparesis; MRI: ring-enhancing lesions; Toxo IgG +.

A) Radiation
B) Pyrimethamine + sulfadiazine + leucovorin
C) Amphotericin
D) Artesunate
E) Ganciclovir
Correct Answer: B

AIDS + ring-enhancing lesions + Toxo IgG + → pyrimethamine + sulfadiazine + leucovorin. TMP-SMX prophylaxis prevents reactivation.
Q12: Babesia
65 y/o splenectomized man from Nantucket with fever, hemolysis; smear: intraerythrocytic rings and Maltese cross.

A) Reduviid bug
B) Ixodes scapularis tick
C) Anopheles mosquito
D) Sandfly
E) Tsetse fly
Correct Answer: B

Babesia microti via Ixodes scapularis. Severe in asplenics. Atovaquone + azithromycin; clinda + quinine if severe.
