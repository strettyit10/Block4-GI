Practice Questions
Q1: APAP overdose timing
A 22-year-old woman arrives 6 hours after ingesting ~30 extra-strength acetaminophen tablets. Currently asymptomatic. BEST next step?
B — Draw 4-hour level + plot on Rumack-Matthew nomogram; start NAC if above treatment line. Phase 1 is classically asymptomatic — normal exam does NOT rule out toxicity. NAC most effective within 8 hours. If timing uncertain or level above line, start NAC empirically.
Q2: Mixed acid-base
45yo M tachypneic with tinnitus, AMS, vomiting. pH 7.48, pCO₂ 22, HCO₃ 16, AG 22. Diagnosis?
C — Salicylates. Classic: primary respiratory alkalosis (medullary stim → tachypnea) + AG metabolic acidosis (uncoupling oxphos → lactate/ketones). Tinnitus = pathognomonic. Treat with NaHCO₃ for urine/serum alkalinization; HD if >100 mg/dL, AMS, renal failure, pulmonary edema.
Q3: Wide-complex tachycardia
30yo F suicide attempt. Somnolent, tachy, hypotensive, dry warm skin, mydriasis. QRS 130 ms + terminal R in aVR. Treatment?
B — Sodium bicarbonate IV. TCA toxicity (anticholinergic + Na-channel block). NaHCO₃: (1) alkalemia reduces drug binding to Na channel, (2) Na load overcomes block. Flumazenil is CONTRAindicated. QRS >100 ms = seizure risk; >160 ms = arrhythmia risk.
Q4: HD indications
Which toxin is MOST effectively removed by HD?
C — Lithium. SLIME — Salicylates, Lithium, Isopropanol, Methanol/Metformin, Ethylene glycol. Dialyzable: low MW, low protein binding, low Vd, water soluble. Lithium meets all. Dig/TCA/CCB all have huge Vd.
Q5: Cherry-red skin
Family of 4 obtunded in winter home with faulty furnace. SpO₂ 99% but cherry-red mucosa + lactic acidosis. Confirmatory test?
C — Co-oximetry (CO-Hb level). Standard pulse ox CANNOT distinguish CO-Hb from O₂-Hb. PaO₂ is normal (dissolved O₂ unchanged). HBO: severe CNS sx, LOC, pregnancy, CO-Hb >25% (>15% if pregnant).
Q6: Toxic alcohol
52yo M intoxicated, no alcohol smell. AG 28, osmolar gap 25. Ca-oxalate crystals on UA. Treatment?
B — Fomepizole IV. Ethylene glycol poisoning. Fomepizole = competitive ADH inhibitor; prevents toxic metabolite formation. Ethanol = backup. Thiamine + pyridoxine shunt to non-toxic metabolites. HD for severe acidosis/renal failure/high levels.
Q7: Charcoal CI
Charcoal is LEAST effective for which ingestion?
D — Lithium. LAME Ca — Lithium, Alcohols, Metals (iron), Electrolytes, Caustics — not adsorbed. Lithium is an ion; WBI preferred (especially SR).
Q8: Cholinergic crisis
40yo farmworker sprayed with pesticide. Copious secretions, miosis, bradycardia, bronchoconstriction, fasciculations. First-line?
A — Atropine IV titrated to dry pulmonary secretions. OP poisoning (AChE inhibition) = cholinergic crisis. Atropine titrated to DRY SECRETIONS (not HR/pupils). Give pralidoxime WITH atropine (not alone) within 24–48h before "aging." Physostigmine would worsen cholinergic excess.
