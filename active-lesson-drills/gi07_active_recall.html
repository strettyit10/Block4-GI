Practice Questions — LO 1–6 Integration

Select the best answer, then reveal the explanation.

Q1. During gastrulation, a group of epiblast cells ingresses most deeply through the primitive streak and displaces the hypoblast. These cells will give rise to which tissue?



C — Definitive endoderm. The deepest ingressing epiblast cells displace the hypoblast and become the definitive endoderm — the future gut tube lining. The hypoblast itself only contributes to the yolk sac, NOT the gut. Sox17 and Nodal (high levels) drive this fate.
Q2. A classmate states: "The hypoblast is the precursor to the gut tube epithelium." Which of the following correctly identifies the error?



B — Hypoblast → yolk sac only. The hypoblast is displaced by epiblast cells ingressing through the primitive streak. It contributes to the secondary yolk sac, not the gut tube. The gut tube lining (definitive endoderm) comes entirely from the epiblast.
Q3. A 2-year-old boy presents with painless rectal bleeding. A Tc-99m pertechnetate scan shows focal radiotracer uptake in the right lower quadrant. This represents ectopic gastric mucosa in a remnant derived from which embryologic structure?



B — Vitelline duct. Meckel's diverticulum is the persistent vitelline (omphalomesenteric) duct. Rule of 2s: 2% of population, 2 feet from ileocecal valve, 2 inches long, often contains 2 types of ectopic tissue (gastric & pancreatic). Ectopic gastric mucosa bleeds painlessly. Tc-99m pertechnetate concentrates in gastric mucosa → hot spot in RLQ.
Q4. Following gastric rotation, the left vagus nerve is carried to which surface of the adult stomach?



C — Anterior surface. The stomach rotates 90° clockwise (ventral view). The original left surface sweeps forward to become the anterior surface, carrying the left vagus → anterior vagal trunk. The right vagus → posterior vagal trunk. Surgically important: vagotomy for peptic ulcer disease must sever both trunks.
Q5. A newborn is found to have intestinal contents herniated through a defect located to the RIGHT of the umbilicus, with no covering membrane and inflamed, matted bowel loops. Which diagnosis best fits?



C — Gastroschisis. Key distinguishing features: (1) defect to the RIGHT of the umbilicus (omphalocele is through the umbilicus), (2) NO membrane covering, (3) bowel is inflamed/thickened from amniotic fluid exposure, (4) usually an isolated defect (no chromosomal association). Omphalocele: through umbilicus, membrane-covered, may contain liver, associated with Trisomy 13/18 and Beckwith-Wiedemann.
Q6. Which of the following structures is considered PRIMARILY retroperitoneal (i.e., never had a mesentery during development)?



D — Kidney. The kidneys (and ureters, adrenals, aorta, IVC, thoracic esophagus, lower 2/3 rectum) are primarily retroperitoneal — they developed in the retroperitoneum and never had a peritoneal covering. The ascending and descending colon, and duodenum D2–D4, are secondarily retroperitoneal — they once had a mesentery but lost it when they fused to the posterior abdominal wall.
Q7. A 3-day-old neonate presents with bilious vomiting. Abdominal X-ray shows two air-fluid levels separated by a radiolucent area (double bubble sign). Which diagnosis and associated chromosomal abnormality are most likely?



B — Duodenal atresia, Trisomy 21. The double bubble sign = dilated stomach + dilated proximal duodenum (two bubbles) with no distal gas, caused by failure of the duodenum to recanalize from its solid cord stage (~week 6). Approximately 30% of cases are associated with Trisomy 21. Bilious vomiting indicates obstruction distal to the ampulla of Vater.
Q8. A patient with internal hemorrhoids above the dentate line asks why they are not painful, unlike the external hemorrhoids below the dentate line. Which of the following correctly explains this difference?



C — Visceral innervation → painless. Above dentate line: hindgut-derived, columnar epithelium, supplied by superior rectal artery (IMA), visceral innervation only → painless (internal hemorrhoids can bleed without pain). Below dentate line: ectoderm-derived, stratified squamous epithelium, supplied by inferior rectal artery (internal pudendal), somatic innervation via pudendal nerve → exquisitely painful.
